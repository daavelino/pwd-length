Please do not use real passwords here.


What makes a good password, mathematically speaking?

  1. An entropy close to a same-size password, considering the alphabet's entropy.
    (The same-size password entropy is the best one can achieve, consider the per-symbol alphabet's entropy.)
  2. A password alphabet large enough to increase its entropy.
    (The length doesn't matter if its password's alphabet is small.)
